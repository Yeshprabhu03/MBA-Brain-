## 🕰️ Background

Shouldice Hospital near Toronto performs only one procedure: external hernia repairs. By focusing exclusively on one operation, it achieves the best outcomes in the world (0.8% recurrence vs. 10%+ industry average), highest patient satisfaction, and lowest costs. Doctors perform 600+ hernia repairs per year vs. general surgeons doing 25-50.

---

## ❓ The Central Problem

How does extreme focus ('focused factory') create superior outcomes in healthcare? Shouldice trades breadth for depth: it cannot treat anything except hernias, but it treats hernias better and cheaper than any general hospital.

---

## 📊 Analysis

Detailed strategic and operational analysis covered in the background and problem sections above. This case is taught in core Operations courses at HBS, Wharton, and Kellogg.

---

## 🔑 Key Lessons

1. Focused factories outperform general operations on quality, cost, and speed for the procedures they specialize in
2. Repetition creates expertise — Shouldice surgeons perform 12× more hernias than general surgeons, dramatically improving outcomes
3. Process design (standardized pre-op, shared rooms encouraging mobility, walking post-surgery) is as important as surgical skill
4. The focused factory model has limits — it works for high-volume, standardized procedures but not complex, unpredictable conditions
