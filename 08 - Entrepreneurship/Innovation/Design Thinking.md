# 🎨 Design Thinking

> **Definition**: A human-centered, iterative problem-solving methodology that emphasizes empathy for the user, radical creativity in ideation, and rapid prototyping of solutions — before committing to implementation.

*Developed by: IDEO (Tim Brown) + Stanford d.school (David Kelley)*
*Fully operationalized at Stanford's Hasso Plattner Institute of Design (d.school)*

---

## 🔑 The Core Mindset Shift

Traditional problem solving:
- Define problem → Analyze → Solve → Implement

Design Thinking:
- **Empathize first** (understand real human needs, not assumptions)
- **Define the right problem** (problem definition is itself creative work)
- **Ideate broadly** (diverge before converging)
- **Prototype cheaply** (make it real quickly, learn fast)
- **Test and iterate** (failure is data)

---

## 📐 The Five Stages

### 1. 🧠 Empathize
*Understand the human experience deeply — not as you imagine it, but as it actually is.*

Methods:
- **Field observation**: Watch people in their natural environment
- **User interviews**: Ask "Why?" 5 times; avoid leading questions
- **'A day in the life'**: Follow a user for a full day
- **Extreme users**: Interview the most demanding and most casual users

**Key output**: Empathy map (what users Say, Think, Do, Feel)

---

### 2. 🎯 Define
*Synthesize observations into a clear problem statement (Point of View / "How Might We").*

Formula:
> **[User] needs [need] because [insight]**

Or "**How Might We** [reframe the challenge]?"

**Good example**:
> "Middle school students in low-income areas need engaging science education because schools lack lab equipment — *How Might We* bring laboratory experiences to resource-constrained classrooms?"

**Bad example**:
> "We need to build an app for science education."

The second skips to a solution. The first opens possibility.

---

### 3. 💡 Ideate
*Generate many ideas — quantity over quality at first.*

Rules of brainstorming:
- **Defer judgment** — No idea is too crazy yet
- **Build on others** ("Yes, and...")
- **Go for volume** — aim for 50+ ideas in 20 min
- **Encourage wild ideas** — push the boundaries
- **One conversation at a time**

**Techniques**:
- Mind mapping
- SCAMPER (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Rearrange)
- Worst possible idea (invert to find the best)
- Brainwriting (silent written ideation to overcome groupthink)

---

### 4. 🔨 Prototype
*Build cheap, fast representations of your top ideas to make them tangible.*

**Mindset**: "Build to think, not to present."

| Fidelity | Method | Cost |
|---------|--------|------|
| Paper prototype | Sketches, sticky notes | Minutes |
| Digital wireframe | Figma, Balsamiq | Hours |
| Physical mock-up | Cardboard, foam core | Hours |
| Functional MVP | Clickable prototype | Days |

**Rule**: Spend no more than necessary to get feedback. Rough is fine.

---

### 5. 🧪 Test
*Get prototypes in front of real users; learn fast.*

- Show, don't explain — let users interact with the prototype naturally
- Observe what confuses them (body language, errors)
- Ask open-ended questions: "What does this do?" not "Is this good?"
- **Fail fast** — killing a bad idea in testing is a win, not a failure
- Iterate: Go back to Empathize or Define if testing reveals a wrong assumption

---

## 🔄 The Process is Non-Linear

Design Thinking isn't a checklist — it's a mindset and loop:

```
Empathize ⟷ Define ⟷ Ideate ⟷ Prototype ⟷ Test
     ↑_____________________________________________↓ (iterate)
```

Teams frequently cycle back based on test feedback.

## 📊 Design Thinking vs. Traditional vs. Lean

| Dimension | Traditional | Design Thinking | Lean Startup |
|-----------|------------|----------------|-------------|
| Starting point | Requirements doc | User empathy | Hypothesis |
| Solution lock | Early | Late | Per experiment |
| User involvement | Low | Very high | Moderate |
| Failure tolerance | Low | High | High |
| Best for | Defined problems | Wicked problems | Business model |
