## 🕰️ Background

AirThread Connections is a regional wireless telecommunications provider being evaluated as an acquisition target by American Cable Communications (ACC). The case requires students to perform a full DCF valuation and comparable company analysis to determine how much ACC should bid.

---

## ❓ The Central Problem

How much should ACC pay for AirThread? Students must build a comprehensive DCF model including: unlevered FCF projections, terminal value calculation (growth vs. exit multiple), appropriate WACC using comparable company betas, and an enterprise-to-equity bridge. The case also requires trading comps analysis as a cross-check on the DCF.

---

## 📊 Analysis

Key modeling decisions: (1) Revenue growth assumptions for wireless subscribers, (2) Operating margin trajectory as the company matures, (3) CapEx intensity for network investment, (4) Terminal growth rate — wireless is maturing, so 2-3% is reasonable, (5) WACC calculation requires finding wireless pure-play comparables, unlevering their betas, and re-levering at AirThread's target capital structure. Synergy analysis: ACC gains bundling (wireless + cable) and infrastructure sharing.

---

## 🔑 Key Lessons

1. **DCF valuation requires coherent assumptions** — growth, margins, and CapEx must tell a consistent story
2. **Terminal value often drives 60-80% of total value** — sensitivity analysis here is critical
3. Synergies should be valued separately from standalone DCF to understand what you're paying for
4. Comparable company analysis serves as a reality check on intrinsic valuation

---

## 🎓 Discussion Questions

1. How sensitive is AirThread's valuation to the terminal growth rate assumption?
2. Should synergies be included in the valuation? If so, who captures the value — buyer or seller?
3. What discount rate is appropriate, and how would you estimate it from comparable companies?
